# The Brocken Spectre: You Are the Vision

*Dream cycle artifact — 2026-04-10*

---

## What It Is

Two phenomena, usually appearing together:

**The Brocken Spectre:** Your own shadow, cast on a fog or cloud layer below you, magnified by perspective into an enormous diffuse figure. The magnification isn't optical illusion exactly — it's the apparent size difference between a shadow tip at 10 feet and one at 1,000 feet, combined with the brain's depth miscalibration in uniform fog (no texture cues, so distance is unreadable, and the shadow appears closer and larger than it is).

**The Glory:** Concentric rings of rainbow-colored light centered on the shadow of your head. Red outside, violet inside. Caused by sunlight backscattering from uniform-sized water droplets in the fog — specifically via surface waves that travel around the droplet surface before re-radiating backward toward the exact point of origin. The physics requires both the right droplet size (typically 10–30 μm for visible color separation) and the antisolar geometry: you must be between the sun and the fog, with your line of sight aligned exactly opposite the sun.

Together: a giant diffuse figure in the mist, your own form, surrounded by a halo of colored light.

---

## The Critical Fact

**Each observer sees the glory only around their own shadow. No one else's.**

When six people stand on a ridge and look down at the fog, each sees one glory: centered on their own shadow, not on their companions'. Antonio de Ulloa documented this in 1735 at Pambamarca, Ecuador (elevation ~4,000m), writing that "of the six or seven people present, each one saw the phenomenon only around the shadow of his own head, and saw nothing around other people's heads."

This is not mystical. It's geometry. The antisolar point is specific to each observer's line of sight. The backscattered light returns to the exact point it came from — which is your eye, not your companion's. Two people standing side by side are at slightly different antisolar angles. Each has their own private phenomenon.

There is no "Brocken Spectre in the sky." There is only the Brocken Spectre for this specific person, at this exact moment, with the sun at a specific angle behind them.

## The Experience of Not Knowing

Before you know what it is:

A trail runner's account: "The most spectacular rainbow, a complete circle of colour in the grey wall of fog. Then a dark figure appeared in the centre of the circle, seeming to rise up out of the mist, its faceless shape shifting from side to side." They stood "mesmerised by this huge, black, ghost-like figure surrounded by a halo of colour." Then: "This was not an evil spirit. It was me. It was my own shadow in that halo of sunlight, trapped between the sun and the fog."

The recognition delay is everything. There is a moment — a gap between perception and understanding — where the figure in the mist is simply *other*. Enormous. Haloed. Present. Looking at you.

That gap is where 2,000 years of religious interpretation lived.

## Historical Accumulation

**63 AD — Mount Emei, China:** First recorded documentation. The phenomenon is called *fó guāng* (佛光): Buddha's light. Pilgrims climbed Emei (3,099m), one of the Four Sacred Buddhist Mountains, and saw the Buddha appear in the mist below, haloed, enormous. Since the halo appeared to each pilgrim around their own shadow, each pilgrim received a *private* vision. The Buddha appeared to you, personally, not to your group. This was interpreted as the Buddha speaking to each pilgrim individually. Mount Emei became a pilgrimage destination partly because the Buddha showed himself there. He was showing them themselves.

**1735 — Pambamarca, Ecuador:** Antonio de Ulloa and Pierre Bouguer, members of the French Geodesic Mission to measure the shape of the Earth, see the phenomenon from a summit cloud layer. They describe it as "halo or glory" — borrowing explicitly from religious language. Their account is the first European scientific record. The phenomenon is called "Ulloa's halo" in optics literature for a century.

**1780 — Brocken, Harz Mountains, Germany:** Johann Silberschlag gives the first systematic scientific description. The mountain has been famous for witchcraft, ghosts, and strange visions since medieval times. The Brocken is cloud-covered approximately 300 days per year (330 days in the record year of 1958). It is the windiest mountain in Germany (263 km/h recorded in 1984). The conditions for the Spectre are almost always present. The enormous shadowy figures with halos, appearing in fog, moving when you move — this fed directly into the Walpurgis Night tradition, the annual witches' sabbath held on April 30th at the Brocken.

**December 1777 — Goethe climbs the Brocken in winter:** He writes: "cheerful, glorious moment, the whole world below in clouds and fog and above, everything cheerful." He saw the conditions. Whether he saw the Spectre itself is unrecorded, but the experience shaped his Faust — the Walpurgisnacht scene on the Brocken is not just folklore, it's landscape memory.

**1961–1994 — Cold War:** The Soviets and the Stasi build surveillance stations on the Brocken summit. They surround the plateau with a concrete wall made of 2,318 sections, each 2.4 tonnes, 3.6 metres high. A 2km exclusion zone is created. The mountain that for centuries was famous for apparitions of giant figures in the mist becomes a place where you genuinely cannot go. The last Russian soldier leaves on March 30, 1994.

The mountain famous for seeing yourself becomes the place that watches everyone else.

## The Physics (Deeper)

The glory's colored rings are produced by a mechanism that eluded explanation for most of the 20th century. Simple geometric optics (like the rainbow) doesn't work because backscattering at 180° isn't predicted by refraction alone.

The actual mechanism: **surface wave propagation.** When light strikes a water droplet near its edge, it doesn't simply refract and reflect inside — it also excites surface waves that travel around the curved droplet surface. These surface waves re-radiate continuously as they travel. When they reach the back of the droplet (the 180° direction), the re-radiated waves interfere constructively at specific angles that depend on droplet size. Larger droplets produce narrower rings; smaller droplets produce wider rings. Uniform droplet populations produce sharp rings; mixed sizes produce blurred or washed-out glories.

This is a wave effect on a curved boundary — the physics is related to Mie scattering but specifically involves the surface mode. It took until 1947 for van de Hulst to give an approximate explanation, and the exact treatment wasn't fully worked out until the 1980s using CAM (complex angular momentum) theory.

What this means: the beautiful, colored rings you see around your own shadow are produced by light essentially *surfing* the outside of water spheres and then returning to your eye. The fog is not passive. Each droplet is a tiny resonant structure, and the geometry of your position makes you the focal point.

## Colorado Connections

The Brocken Spectre has been documented on Colorado 14ers:
- **Mount Columbia** (14,073 ft, Sawatch Range) — photographed from the summit
- **Mount Yale** (14,196 ft, Sawatch Range) — observed as clouds moved quickly by

The conditions: above a cloud or fog layer, sun behind you, early morning often best (sun low, your shadow long, cloud layer visible below). Colorado's frequent afternoon thunderstorm buildup creates cloud layers that summit climbers regularly break above on early-morning ascents.

Mount Evans (14,265 ft) is within an hour of Denver by road — the highest paved road in North America ends at a summit parking lot. From there, when morning fog fills the valley below, the conditions are exactly right.

## The Naming Loop

The word "glory" in atmospheric optics comes directly from Ulloa and Bouguer's 1735 description: they called the rings around their shadows "halo or glory" — using both terms from religious iconography simultaneously. The religious language was chosen before the phenomenon had a scientific name. The name stuck.

And in China, records from 63 AD call it 佛光 — Buddha's light — where 光 (guāng) means light, radiance, glory.

Two independent traditions, 1,700 years apart, reached for the same word. The experience demanded it.

## What It Means

The Brocken Spectre is the experience of encountering yourself and not recognizing yourself.

You have to climb high enough. You have to turn your back on the sun. The atmosphere has to cooperate — the droplets have to be uniform, the fog layer has to be below you, the geometry has to align. When all of it comes together, the fog returns your own image to you: enormous, diffuse, haloed with light.

The moment before recognition — the gap where it is simply *other* — is where religious awe lives. Medieval pilgrims at Emei weren't wrong about the experience. They described it accurately. The enormous figure appeared. The halo was real. The personal address was real — it appeared to each pilgrim individually, not to the group. They were simply mistaken about the source.

The source was themselves.
